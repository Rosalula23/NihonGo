Location

Describing Where Things Are

- To ask for and give basic location:

X は　どこ　ですか。
Where is X?

ｘ　は　あそこ・そこ・ここ　です。
X is over there/there/here.

- To give location relative to another object:

ｘ　は　Ｙ　の　Location です。
X is located __ relative to Y.

- To describe where a verb took place:
Use the above phrases as a noun phrase along with で.

Xは　Yのlocation で　verb です。　
X did verb at __ relative to Y.

Examples:

銀行は図書館のとなりです。
The bank is next to the library.

私は店の前で一時間を待ちました。
I waited in front of the store for 1 hour.

このレストランはスーパーとコンビニの間です。
That restaurant is between the supermarket and the convenience store.

Frequency

Frequency adverbs describe how often a verb is done.

Adverbs describing frequency require a positive verb.
Adverbs describing infrequency require a negative verb.

Examples:

私は時々図書館に行きます。
I often go to the library.

毎日漢字を勉強します。
I study kanji every day.

全然テルビを見ません。
I do not watch TV at all.

Adjectives

Adjectives modify nouns

Type Dictionary Example Meaning
い 面白い 面白い映画 an interesting movie
な きれいな きれいな写真 a beautiful picture
Irregular 格好いい 格好いい先生 a good-looking teacher

To modify nouns, put the dictionary form of the adjective in front of the noun.
To describe something as being (adjective), use conjugations here

You can use 好きな and 嫌いな as adjectives to mean "my favorite" and "my least favorite"

Examples:

私の好きな本
my favorite book

私の嫌いな映画
my least favorite movie

て form is used to connect two adjectives in a single sentence.

NOUNは ADJて、ADJ です。
This thing is X and X.

Examples:

あの店の食べ物は安くて、おいしいです。
The food at that shop is cheap and delicious.

山下先生は日本人で、五十歳ぐらいです。
Professor Yamashita is Japanese and about 50 years old.

と only connects nouns, so cannot be used here.

Qualifying Nouns

Describing someone or something as "A person/thing that is X"

Basic

Put the adjective in front of the noun.

Examples:

面白い人
An interesting person

髪が長い人
A person with long hair

青い車
A blue car

Verb in progress

To describe things on the basis of their current actions, use
verb + ている + noun
This is frequently used to identify people.

Examples:

ゆいさんはあそこで本を読んでいる人です。
Yui is the person who is reading a book over there.

あそこで写真を撮っている学生はロバートさんです。
The student who is taking pictures over there is Robert.

Comparisions

For two items:

Aのほうが Bより Xです。
A is more X than B.

Examples:

中国のほうが日本より大きいです。
China is larger than Japan.

日本よりカナダのほうが寒いです。
Canada is colder than Japan.

The order the two items to be compared are placed in doesn't matter. It only matters which is attached to のほうが(more) and which is attached to より(than).

For three or more items:

AとBとCと の中で A・B・C がいちばん Xです。
Between A, B, and C, A/B/C is the most X.

Examples:

カナダとフランスと日本の中で、カナダがいちばん寒いです。
Between Canada, France, and Japan, Canada has the coldest climate.

季節の中で、秋がいちばん好きです。
Of all the seasons, I like fall the best.

In questions:

For two items:

AとBと どちらのほうが・どっちのほうが Xですか。
Between A and B, which is more X?

To answer:

A/BのほうがXです。
A/B is more X.

For three or more items:

AとBとCと の中で (question word) がいちばん Xですか。
Between A, B, and C, which is the most X?

To answer:

A・B・CがいちばんXです。
A/B/C is the most X.

For three or more items, normal question words are used in place of どちらのほうが・どっちのほうが

Examples:

日本とカナダとどちらのほうが寒いですか。
Which is colder, Japan or Canada?

カナダのほうが寒いです。
Canada is colder.

カナダとフランスと日本の中で、どこがいちばん寒いですか。
Between Canada, France, and Japan, which country has the coldest climate?

カナダがいちばん寒いと思います。
I think Canada is coldest.

Too Much

To say "too much" or "to excess":

Verbs

Use verb stem + 過ぎる (る)

Adjectives

Use adjective stem (drop い・な) + すぎる

Examples:

早く起き過ぎました。
I got up too early.

この本は高すぎます。
That book is too expensive.

この町は静か過ぎます。
This town is too quiet.

過ぎる is only used when something is too much for comfort or propriety. It suggests that you do no welcome the excess. If you want to say something is in a high degree (such as "too kind"), use modifiers like とても or すごく.
